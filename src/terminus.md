You know, I've been at it for a couple of hours trying to figure out what to write for my Terminus, and I have pivoted from multiple starting points so far. First, I had imagined writing a monolithic magnum opus. Then I thought about dividing it into books, with each disseminating a central idea, and the series lacking a conclusion which represents the ultimate lack of assigned purpose in life. Then I thought about dividing my ideas further into aphorisms: short essays on noteworthy topics, when those combined create a clearer picture to allow the reader to form connections between them as they see fit. Now I'm not sure of what I'm going to do, and I suppose that makes sense. Writing a program, you don't know the entire software architecture from the get-go. You prototype, you test, and then you re-prototype through successive iteration. I'm sure the most optimal structure will be able to be determined, but as of right now I am more focused on the content.
